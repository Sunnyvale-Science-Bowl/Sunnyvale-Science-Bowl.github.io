Overview

What is Science Bowl?

Science Bowl is a competition run by the Department of Energy, a government department that also manages the United States’ energy supply and runs the national labs. Science Bowl is a team-based verbal buzzer competition. Teams of 5 students compete at the regional competition against other schools in our area: Miller, Stratford, and more.

In the first half of the day, the “morning”, teams compete in round robin divisions to determine who makes it to the “afternoon” rounds. After lunch, teams compete in a double elimination bracket to determine the winner. The winning team gets a trip to Washington DC, where they face teams from across the US at nationals.

There are 6 main subjects in the competition:

  • Math
  • Earth and Space
  • Energy
  • Physical Science
  • General Science
  • Life Science

What do we do in Science Bowl Club?

We do a couple of different things throughout the school year.

  • Weekly practices to prepare for the regional competition
  • Buzzer practice and quizzes
  • Host speakers and activities
  • Science Fair

History

Science Bowl was started by Gilbert Feng around 2014, and was mainly parent led. As with many clubs, the club shut down. In 2022, Walter and Peter revived the club, and from 2023 onwards we are aiming for a high school student-led club, with parents and teachers advising.

We have achieved some success at the regional competition, but have never made nationals. Here are some of our top performances:

  • 3rd place? (2018)
  • 2nd place (2019)
  • 4th place (2020)
